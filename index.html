Is Learning a Second Language Still Necessary in the Age of Artificial Intelligence?

Duration
10 – 15 minutes
Format
Anonymous survey
Participation
Voluntary
Principal Investigator
WONG Kwan Yiu David
Research Aim

This study investigates whether university students understand content delivered by instructors and how this affects their learning attitude, motivation, behaviour, and action. The project is supervised by Dr. James LENZER and conducted by WONG Kwan Yiu David, Department of Science and Environmental Studies, EdUHK.

Your Participation
Answer approximately 20 questions on your habits of using real-time language translation tools in class. This is a one-off participation of approximately 10–15 minutes.
Benefits & Incentive

You will have the opportunity to self-reflect on your use of translation tools in your learning process. A spending voucher (e.g. supermarket, bookstore) will be provided as a token of appreciation.

Dissemination

Results may be shared within the department and, if findings merit it, published in academic journals or presented at educational conferences.

Potential Risks

None. There are no known risks or discomfort associated with participation in this study.

Confidentiality

All information will remain strictly confidential, identifiable only by codes known to the researcher. Your personal details will not be revealed in any publication or report.

Voluntary Participation

Your participation is entirely voluntary. You have the right to withdraw at any time without negative consequences. Information obtained may be used in future research while fully retaining your right to privacy.
